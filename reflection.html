Speed Beats Perfection

Click to expand

In entrepreneurship, the ability to rapidly prototype and iterate based on feedback is more valuable than writing perfect code.

Applied: When requirements changed after CA1, I could pivot quickly because I hadn't over-engineered the initial prototype.

Customer Validation Drives Everything

Click to expand

Technical decisions must be subordinate to validated customer needs, not personal preferences or technical curiosity.

Applied: When A/B tests showed parents preferred structured summaries, I prioritized that over the analytics dashboard I wanted to build.

Prototyping is a distinct discipline with different success metrics: learning and validation, not code quality or scalability.

Applied: Used mock data and simplified algorithms to demonstrate concepts quickly, saving weeks of development time.

Communicate in Business Terms

Click to expand

In entrepreneurial teams, technical work must be constantly translated into customer value and business impact.

Applied: Instead of saying "I implemented the database schema," I learned to say "Parents can now track their child's progress, addressing their trust concerns."

Requirements will change. Build with flexibility in mind and treat uncertainty as a feature, not a bug.
